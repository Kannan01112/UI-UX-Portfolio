About Myself:

Hi everyone. My name is Kannan. I am currently a student in SUSS. I made this blog to share my insights about Hanoi during my travels there. I hope you find it helpful in planning your own holiday trip to Hanoi.

Why visit Hanoi?

Reason 1:

Hanoi is rich in culture. Many of Vietnam's cultural sites are situated in Hanoi.

Reason 2:

Hanoi is great for photography. It is filled with natural beauty that is Instagram-worthy.

Reason 3:

Hanoi has unique and exquisite foods that are a must-try for foodies.


Recommended places to visit in Hanoi



President Ho Chi Minh Museum/Mausoleum


My Experience:

The Museum gave me an insight into Vietnam's history that I did not know about before. It was my first time seeing a public figure frozen in time in a Mausoleum. The towering bronze statue within the Museum was a sight to behold. Despite being a museum, it never felt dull. The sheer number of artifacts from the past to look through keeps you occupied enough.

    • Temple of Literature


    My Experience:

    The Temple of Literature was a serene place to visit. The gardens right after the entrance takes your mind away from the hustle and bustle of city life. Further in, you will see the temple, which contains stone steles preserved carefully. They still retain the scholars’ names carved into them centuries ago. I could easily spend hours soaking in the peaceful environment at the temple.

    • Hoan Kiem Lake


    My Experience:

    Hoan Kiem Lake is the most talked about tourist place for a reason. Surrounding the lake are countless food options. The lake itself is beautiful. The lake's colour changes depending on the sunlight. If you do visit the lake, watching the water puppet show is a must. The puppeteers beautifully choreograph the puppets to tell the folk tale of Hoan Kiem Lake. The traditional music and the beautiful story telling kept me immersed throughout the show.

    • Recommended Hanoi Restaurants to try



    Bun Cha Ta Restaurant


    My Experience:

    Bun Cha Ta is a must-try when in Hanoi. It is located in the old quarter district. It is very convenient to drop by for your meals from hotels in the old quarter district. The meatballs in the dish are savoury but not too strong in flavour. The portion is plenty and will keep you satiated.


    Visit Restaurant


    Banh Mi 25 Restaurant


    My Experience:

    My personal favourite dish is Banh Mi. The Banh Mi is Vietnam's take on the sandwich. Banh Mi 25 secret sauce recipe sets apart its flavour from other Banh Mi stores. The restaurant is right next to Hoan Kiem Lake. You can grab a Banh Mi to go and enjoy it while glancing upon Hoan Kiem Lake's beauty.
